The problem

Financial data on a plain USB is a compliance liability.

Tax returns, audit packages, and financial statements carry some of the most sensitive personal data a client will ever hand you. Delivering them on an unencrypted USB — or over email or a generic cloud link — puts that data, and your firm's compliance obligations, at risk.

Compliance angle

Supports GDPR Article 32 requirements.

Article 32 calls for "appropriate technical and organisational measures," including encryption of personal data. Delivering pre-encrypted files on physical media, with the decryption password shared through a separate channel, is one concrete way to support that requirement.
